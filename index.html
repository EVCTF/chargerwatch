Section 3 · The three standards we track

What Maryland regulators are required to enforce

Reliability: the 97% uptime standard

Maryland law (HB 834, 2023) requires investor-owned utility chargers to maintain 97% annual uptime, matching the federal NEVI standard. The PSC receives quarterly reports. As of this writing, no utility has faced a financial penalty for missing the target.

See reliability cases →

Weights & measures: accurate billing

The Maryland Department of Agriculture's Weights and Measures Division regulates whether chargers are registered, labeled correctly, and bill accurately per kilowatt-hour. A charger can meet the uptime standard and still have a weights and measures problem. These are separate agencies and separate legal hooks.

See weights & measures cases →

Tax collection: EV drivers pay too

Sales tax on EV charging sessions flows to Maryland's Transportation Trust Fund. Chargers that collect $0.00 in sales tax on sessions may be exploiting a pricing loophole that disadvantages compliant operators and denies the state revenue it is owed.

Report a tax issue →

Section 4 · Featured case files

Documented cases with formal filings

Reliability · BGE · Anne Arundel County

BWI Airport Cell Phone Lot: persistent derating and repeated outages

Statistical analysis of full-year 2025 session data showed near-continuous derating on two connectors, a 27.6% fleet-wide zero-kWh failure rate, and 681 persistent failure runs. A complete outage was documented Memorial Day 2026.

Under review · PSC Case No. 9478
Weights & measures · Pepco · Prince George's County

Bladensburg Town Hall DCFC: forensic evidence of systematic overbilling

Forensic electrical analysis showed a roughly 5:1 discrepancy between current billed and current delivered. A separate SWTCH app anomaly allowed CHAdeMO activation while CCS was displayed. Exhibits filed in PSC Case No. 9478; matter escalated to the Maryland AG.

Under review · PSC Case No. 9478
Reliability · Pepco · Prince George's County

Clinton Park and Ride: total failure exceeding ten weeks

Analysis of more than 112,000 PHI DCFC sessions across 470+ stations identified a sustained total outage at this station lasting over ten weeks, a distinct failure signature not consistent with routine maintenance.

Open · PSC Case No. 9478
Weights & measures · ChargePoint · Anne Arundel County

Hotel at Arundel Preserve: undisclosed per-session fee

A $0.25 per-session service fee was not reflected in the posted per-kWh price at the time of the complaint. A weights and measures complaint was filed with MDA and validated by an inspector.

Resolved · MDA W&M complaint

About this site

Who we are and how we work

ChargerWatch.org is a project of the EV Charging Task Force (EVCTF), a Maryland-based volunteer citizen watchdog group, published in partnership with PlugInSites.org. The EVCTF is an active intervenor in Maryland PSC proceedings and a participant in the PSC EV Work Group.

Every station on the map is drawn from a verified inventory of Maryland DC fast chargers. Issues are classified by evidence tier: filed and verified (formal PSC, MDA, or AG filing), documented pattern (data-backed but not yet formally filed), or reported and unverified (community tip with supporting evidence, pending review). We show our work and we say what we don't know.

The EVCTF has been quoted in the Washington Post, Baltimore Sun, and Politico. Our founder has 14 years of EV driving experience across 48 states and DC.
